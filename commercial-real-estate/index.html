Core Capital Solutions for Commercial Real Estate

EPOCH structures commercial real estate financing through four core capital solutions designed to align with asset performance, sponsor objectives, and transaction structure.

Bridge Financing

Bridge financing supports transitional and event-driven real estate transactions including acquisitions, lease-up strategies, recapitalizations, and refinancing events.

Facilities are secured by commercial real estate assets and underwritten based on current asset performance, forward net operating income projections, and sponsor execution capability.

Structured bridge capital provides certainty during periods when conventional lenders may delay or decline financing due to transitional asset characteristics.

Acquisition Financing

Acquisition financing supports sponsors acquiring stabilized or transitional commercial real estate assets. Facilities are structured based on asset-level performance, market conditions, and sponsor equity commitment.

Underwriting incorporates in-place lease income, tenant credit quality, market supply-demand dynamics, and projected operating performance.

Acquisition financing structures provide sponsors with flexible leverage aligned with asset fundamentals and defined investment timelines.

Transitional Asset Financing

Many commercial real estate investments involve assets undergoing operational improvement or repositioning prior to stabilization. Transitional asset financing supports sponsors executing value creation strategies across institutional property types.

Facilities are structured around projected stabilization milestones, forward net operating income growth, and clearly defined exit strategies.

This approach allows sponsors to implement operational improvements while maintaining access to structured capital.

  • check_circleLease-up of newly delivered properties
  • check_circleRenovation and repositioning strategies
  • check_circleTenant reconfiguration programs
  • check_circleRedevelopment of underperforming assets
  • check_circleStabilization prior to permanent financing

Portfolio Recapitalization Financing

Portfolio recapitalization financing supports real estate sponsors seeking to optimize capital structures across multi-asset portfolios.

Portfolio-level financing evaluates combined asset performance, tenant diversification, geographic exposure, and sponsor operational capability.

This structure enables sponsors to unlock capital while maintaining long-term ownership positions across institutional real estate assets.

Commercial Real Estate Assets We Finance

Middle market commercial real estate financing is primarily directed toward established institutional property sectors with predictable cash flow characteristics, professional management structures, and scalable tenant demand.

business Office

Office

Office properties include buildings leased to corporate tenants for professional, administrative, and business operations.

Typical characteristics include:

  • check_circleClass A or Class B office buildings
  • check_circleCentral business district or major suburban markets
  • check_circleDiversified tenant rosters or credit tenants
  • check_circleLong-term lease structures

Financing commonly supports acquisitions, refinancing transactions, tenant improvement programs, and lease-up repositioning strategies.

apartment Multifamily

Multifamily

Multifamily properties consist of professionally managed apartment communities with stabilized occupancy and consistent rental income.

Typical institutional multifamily characteristics include:

  • check_circleApartment communities with 100 or more units
  • check_circleGarden-style, mid-rise, or high-rise developments
  • check_circleStrong rent collections and stable occupancy
  • check_circleInstitutional ownership or professional management

Financing is frequently used for acquisitions, refinancing, value-add renovation strategies, and portfolio recapitalizations.

warehouse Industrial

Industrial

Industrial real estate includes warehouse, logistics, and distribution facilities supporting modern supply chains and e-commerce fulfillment networks.

Common property formats include:

  • check_circleDistribution centers
  • check_circleFlex industrial properties
  • check_circleLast-mile logistics facilities
  • check_circleLight manufacturing or assembly space

Industrial properties are currently among the most financeable asset classes due to strong tenant demand and long-term lease structures.

storefront Retail

Retail

Institutional retail properties typically consist of well-located retail centers anchored by national or strong regional tenants.

Typical formats include:

  • check_circleGrocery-anchored shopping centers
  • check_circleLifestyle retail centers
  • check_circlePower centers
  • check_circleNet-leased retail portfolios

Lenders generally favor properties with essential retail tenants, high traffic locations, and strong tenant credit profiles.

hotel Hospitality

Hospitality

Hospitality assets include branded hotels with established operating history and professional management.

Typical hospitality property types include

  • check_circleSelect-service hotels
  • check_circleLimited-service hotels
  • check_circleFull-service branded hotels

Financing considerations include brand affiliation, revenue per available room performance, location demand drivers, and operating history.

How is leverage determined in middle market commercial real estate transactions?

expand_more

Leverage levels are determined based on asset quality, in-place and projected net operating income, debt yield coverage, and market liquidity conditions. Loan-to-value metrics are evaluated alongside debt service coverage and exit strategy visibility.

add

How are transitional assets underwritten?

expand_more

Underwriting incorporates both current property performance and forward projections. Lease-up assumptions, tenant rollover schedules, capital improvement requirements, and market absorption trends are analyzed to determine stabilization feasibility.

How is capital structured across the real estate capital stack?

expand_more

Middle market real estate transactions may include layered capital structures such as senior secured facilities, mezzanine capital, and working capital components. Structural subordination and collateral priority are evaluated to align risk allocation with expected asset performance.
